How Long Does It Take to Import a Car from Japan to Australia?

Typical timeframes for the import process — and why we give you a clear expectation when you enquire.

Summary

The time to import a car from Japan to Australia typically includes: sourcing the vehicle (e.g. at auction), compliance and certification (e.g. RAWS), shipping to your chosen Australian port, and customs clearance. Total timeframes often fall in a range of several weeks to a few months depending on the vehicle, auction schedule, compliance workload and shipping. We outline expected timelines when you enquire.

Main Stages and Typical Durations

Sourcing can take from a few days to several weeks depending on whether the car is already identified or we are searching (e.g. at auction). Compliance and certification — including RAWS where required — typically take a few weeks once the vehicle is in Australia or at an approved facility. Shipping from Japan to an Australian port is usually in the range of a few weeks depending on route and sailings. Customs and clearance add a few days to a couple of weeks. These are typical ranges; actual timeframes depend on the specific vehicle, workshop availability and shipping schedule. We do not guarantee fixed timelines but we give you a clear expectation when we quote and will keep you updated through the process.

How We Keep It Efficient

Having team on the ground in Japan helps with sourcing and inspection, so we can move quickly from your go-ahead to purchase. We then manage compliance and shipping in sequence so there are no unnecessary delays. For Japanese car imports to Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra or Hobart, we will outline the expected timeline when you enquire.

FAQ

Can you give a fixed delivery date?

We give you an expected timeline based on your vehicle and destination. Exact dates depend on sourcing, compliance and shipping; we keep you updated as the job progresses.

Does bulk import take longer?

Bulk orders involve multiple vehicles, so the overall process can take longer, but we run them in parallel where possible and will give you a timeline when you enquire.
