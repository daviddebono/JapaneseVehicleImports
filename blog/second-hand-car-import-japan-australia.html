Second-Hand Car Import from Japan to Australia

What you need to know about importing a used (second-hand) Japanese car — eligibility, compliance and using a broker.

Summary

Most Japanese cars imported to Australia are second-hand (used). Eligibility depends on the model and Australian rules. A broker confirms the vehicle can be imported, manages compliance and certification (e.g. RAWS), and coordinates shipping and delivery. We have team in Japan who inspect and purchase from auction on your behalf. We service Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra and Hobart.

Why Second-Hand Japanese Cars?

Japan has a large market for used cars, often well maintained and with detailed auction or dealer history. Many models are eligible for import to Australia. The key is to confirm eligibility before you buy and to have compliance and certification handled so the car can be registered here. As your Japanese car import broker we do both: we check the vehicle for Australian compliance and manage the full process from Japan to your chosen location in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra or Hobart.

Using a Broker for Used Imports

A broker saves you from navigating eligibility, RAWS, shipping and customs yourself. We also have team on the ground in Japan who can source the car (including at auction), inspect it and purchase on your behalf — so you are not buying completely unseen. Tell us what you want and we will confirm eligibility and outline the process.

FAQ

Are most Japanese imports used cars?

Yes. The majority of Japanese vehicles imported to Australia are second-hand. We confirm each one for Australian eligibility and manage compliance.

Do you buy from auction?

Yes. Our team in Japan inspects and purchases from auction on your behalf for used cars (and vans and trucks).
