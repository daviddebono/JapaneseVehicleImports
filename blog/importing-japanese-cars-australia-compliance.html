Why Compliance Matters

Australia has strict rules about which vehicles can be imported and registered. Not every Japanese car is eligible. Before you commit to a purchase, you need to know that the vehicle can be imported to Australia and what compliance and certification steps are required. Getting this wrong can mean extra cost, delay or, in the worst case, a vehicle that cannot be registered here.

Australian Design Rules and Eligibility

Imported vehicles must comply with Australian Design Rules (ADRs) applicable at the time of manufacture or as determined by the federal regulator. Eligibility depends on the specific model, variant and build date. A broker who specialises in Japanese car imports will check each vehicle for Australian compliance before you buy. That way you are not left with a car that cannot be imported.

RAWS and Certification

Where a vehicle requires modification or certification to meet Australian standards, it typically must go through a RAWS (Registered Automotive Workshop Scheme) approved workshop. The workshop issues the documentation needed for registration. As your broker, we manage this process: we ensure the vehicle can be imported and we coordinate all compliance and certification so the car lands ready for registration (subject to your state or territory requirements).

FAQ

Can any Japanese car be imported to Australia?

No. Eligibility depends on the specific model and Australian rules at the time. We check each vehicle for Australian compliance before you commit.

What is RAWS?

RAWS (Registered Automotive Workshop Scheme) is the scheme under which approved workshops modify or certify vehicles to meet Australian standards. We coordinate this as part of the import process.
